About

Leading people strategy in the luxury sector has taught me that exceptional products are only born from exceptional teams. As Chief People Officer at Élysée Maison, I've spent the past 12 years cultivating environments where craftsmanship meets innovation, and where every individual feels empowered to contribute their unique artistry to our collective vision.

My philosophy centers on the idea that organizational culture, like fine craftsmanship, cannot be rushed. It requires patience, attention to detail, and a deep respect for both tradition and evolution. I've worked across Asia, Europe, and North America, learning that while business practices may differ, the fundamental human need for purpose, belonging, and growth remains universal.

In an industry often defined by its heritage, I've championed progressive approaches to talent development, diversity, and wellness—not as contradictions to luxury values, but as essential expressions of them. True luxury, I believe, begins with how we treat the people who create it.

My leadership draws from both Eastern and Western traditions. From tai chi, I've learned the power of intentional action. From meditation, the value of clear presence. These practices have shaped not just my personal approach, but how I guide our organization through periods of transformation and growth.

I find peace in movement, clarity in stillness.
